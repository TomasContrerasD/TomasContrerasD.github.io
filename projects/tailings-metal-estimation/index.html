Estimation of Heavy Metal Concentrations in Tailings Deposits using Multispectral Satellite Imagery

Adolfo Ibáñez University  ·  Last updated: January 27, 2025

Description

The work focuses on estimating concentrations of heavy metals — Cu, Ni, Zn, Co, and As — in tailings deposits located in the Coquimbo Region of Chile, using multispectral satellite imagery as the primary data source.

This repository contains the code and data used in the Master of Science in Data Science thesis titled "Estimation of heavy metal concentrations in tailings deposits from multispectral satellite imagery", carried out by Tomás Ignacio Contreras Delporte at Universidad Adolfo Ibáñez.

View on GitHub

Objective

The main objective of this project is to evaluate the viability of using satellite imagery to estimate heavy metal concentrations in mining tailings deposits. Through regression analysis and the use of various statistical and machine learning models, the aim is to identify patterns in spectral data to produce accurate estimations.

Methodology

  1. Study Area: Coquimbo Region, Chile, with a focus on active, inactive, and abandoned tailings deposits.

    Tailings deposits in the Coquimbo Region, Chile.

  2. Data Acquisition:
    • Satellite Data: Multispectral imagery obtained from Landsat 8 satellites.
    • Metal Concentration Data: Historical records provided by Sernageomin.
  3. Data Preprocessing: Satellite imagery was preprocessed to ensure spectral data quality, applying geometric and radiometric corrections.
  4. Predictive Modelling: Use of statistical models including Partial Least-Squares Regression (PLSR), Generalized Linear Models (GLM), and Generalized Additive Models (GAM) to estimate heavy metal concentrations.

Results

The Partial Least-Squares Regression (PLSR) model proved most effective for estimating Zn concentrations, while the Generalized Linear Model (GLM) showed superior performance for metals such as Ni, Cu, and As.

The methodology revealed that metal concentrations themselves are not the determining factor in estimation accuracy. Instead, the analysis highlighted the critical importance of correlations between spectral bands and the influence of extreme values in the predictive models.

Technical Stack
